Alan Turing's Personal Life

Though celebrated for his brilliance, Turing faced severe persecution for his homosexuality, which was illegal in Britain at the time. He was convicted in 1952 and subjected to chemical castration, a punishment that ultimately led to his tragic death in 1954.

A Tragic End

Despite his monumental contributions to humanity, Turing's life was marred by discrimination. In 2013, he was posthumously pardoned by the British government.
